Korean and Japanese Fashion

Japanese and Korean fashion are two of the most prominent influences in the world of contemporary fashion, each with its unique style and distinctive characteristics. Both have gained global popularity thanks to their creativity, innovation and ability to mix tradition with modern trends.

J-FASHION

Harajuku Style

Mix of vibrant colors, excessive layers, and flashy accessories.

Gyaru Style

People wearing school jackets, pleated skirts, and turtleneck sweaters (popular in K-dramas like "Extraordinary Attorney Woo").

Mori Kei

Loose fittings, natural fabrics and earth tones. Inspired by rural life and Ghibli.

Japanese Techwear

Functional clothing with tactical pockets and resistant materials.

Modern Kimono

Mix of vibrant colors, excessive layers, and flashy accessories.

K-FASHION

Ulzzang Style

Flawless skin, simple but well-cared for clothing, natural hair, and minimalist accessories.

Korean Academy

People wearing school jackets, pleated skirts, and turtleneck sweaters (popular in K-dramas like "Extraordinary Attorney Woo").

Genderless Fashion

Unisex clothing: overshirts, baggy pants, and platform shoes (brands like Andersson Bell).

Y2K Revival

Cargo pants, crop tops, and neon colors (groups like NewJeans and BLACKPINK).

Clean Fit

Minimalism with oversized but structured silhouettes (neutral tones and premium materials).

Fashion tips to apply in your daily life

Here are some practical tips for incorporating Japanese and Korean fashion into your daily life:

Basics

Basics like white shirts, black pants, and leather jackets are versatile and can be combined in many ways to create different outfits.

Jackets

A jacket can add a touch of sophistication to any outfit.

Pants

Pants are versatile and can be worn on different occasions, from a formal event to a casual

Accesories

Accessories can add a personal touch to your outfit and make it stand out.
